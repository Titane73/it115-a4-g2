Experience Seattle's Diversity in Dining

Whether you want to dine on flavor-packed dishes or sip small-batch spirits, Seattle is the place to taste your heart out. Local celebrity chefs like Tom Douglas, Ethan Stowell, and Renee Erickson infuse the city with their creative flavors, while a number of Washington wineries, breweries, coffee roasters, cideries, soda makers, and distilleries make it easy to always drink like a local. Sink your teeth into juicy burgers, get a sugar fix at a beloved Seattle bakery, or relax at an indie coffee shop. The options are endlessly delicious.

The City With The Best Fans!

Seattle Seahawks

Football at CenturyLink Field set a World Record for loudest crowd roar during a 2013 Seattle Seahawks game. The Seahawks are a professional American football team based in Seattle, Washington that compete in the National FootBall Conference West division. Seahawks fans have been referred to as the “12th Man”, “12th Fan” or “12s”. In 2014, the Seahawks won their franchise’s first Super Bowl Championship, defeating the Denver Broncos 43-8.


Seattle Seahawks

Seattle Sounders FC

Soccer supporters chant, sing, and cheer throughout Sounders FC games. Sounders FC, an American professional soccer club based in Seattle, WA that competes as a member of the Western Conference of Major League Soccer. The Sounders play their home league matches at CenturyLink Field. In 2019, the Sounders won against Toronto FC in the MLS Cup Final and brought home a second MLS Cup title.


Seattle Sounders FC

Seattle Mariners

Baseball fans love the classic ballpark atmosphere during Seattle Mariners games. Seattle Mariners, an American professional baseball team based in Seattle, Washington that competes in Major League Baseball as a member club of the American League West division. The Mariners play their home league matches at T-Mobile Park.


Seattle Mariners

Seattle Storm

Seattle Storm is a women’s professional basketball team based in Seattle, WA. The Storm are a must-watch team at Alaska Airlines Arena at the University of Washington, the Storm’s temporary home while the Seattle Center arena is under renovation. This year, the Storm closed out the top-ranked Las Vegas Aces in three games en route to their fourth championship.

Something for Everyone

The Emerald City

Bustling city, expansive beaches, or evergreen covered mountains, Seattle has everything a tourist could ask for. With the famous Space Needle, unique and historic neighborhoods, and SeaTac international airport at the Southern end, the Emerald City is a popular vacation destination with plenty to offer the first time visitor, or the regular vacationer.

Logs to Tech

Seattle is the largest city in the Pacific Northwest, with a population of almost 4 million. Named after Chief Si’ahl of the Duwamish and Suquamish tribes, it is a city full of rich history. Starting as a logging colony, the city has developed into one of the leading tech cities in the country, hosting the headquarters to the tech giants Amazon and Microsoft, among many others.

Capitol Hill

As one of the most progressive cities in the country, many in the LGBTQ community have found home in the unique neighborhood of Capitol Hill. A neighborhood full of bars, music, cafes and restaurants, visitors can easily spend the whole day in Capitol Hill and meet many of the great people that call this neighborhood home.

And Coffee!

A city known for its coffee, Seattle is home to the largest chain of coffee shops in the world, Starbucks. At the famous Pike Place Market, one can find themself at the original Starbucks location. Be prepared to wait, as this popular spot regularly has a long line waiting to see where the siren got her start.

Introduction:

The Space Needle is an observation tower in Seattle, Washington, United States. Considered to be an icon of the city and the Pacific Northwest, it has been designated a Seattle landmark. Located in the Lower Queen Anne neighborhood, it was built in the Seattle Center for the 1962 World's Fair, which drew over 2.3 million visitors. Nearly 20,000 people a day used its elevators during the event.
